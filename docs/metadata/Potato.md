# Potato

**Potato** _(Solanum tuberosum)_, often called the "King of Vegetables," is a herbaceous perennial in the family Solanaceae. Native to the Andes of South America, it is composed of starchy, edible tubers and has become one of the most widely cultivated and consumed food crops on Earth. Renowned for its high caloric value, adaptability, and nutritional richness, it is a cornerstone of global food security and a vital cash crop for millions of farming families.

Potato _(Solanum tuberosum, family Solanaceae)_ is the silent workhorse of Bangladesh's winter agriculture — a crop that transformed nutrition, livelihoods, and agricultural landscapes across the country's northern and central districts. Grown extensively in Munshiganj, Bogra, Rangpur, and Dinajpur, it is the world's fourth-most important food crop after rice, wheat, and maize. The Bangladesh Agricultural Research Institute (BARI), through its Potato Research Centre (PRC), has developed high-yielding and disease-resistant varieties suited to the country's cool, dry rabi season. Because potatoes are propagated through tubers rather than true seed, they carry pathogens forward from one generation to the next, making certified, disease-free seed stock the single most critical factor in disease management. Fungal blights, bacterial wilts, viruses, and soil-borne nematodes all threaten yield — but with proper rotation, chemical management, and resistant varieties, Bangladesh's potato farmers continue to secure one of the most productive harvests in South Asia.

# Potato (_Solanum tuberosum_) — Diseases, Symptoms & Management

> Potato is a critical food crop of Bangladesh and South Asia. This document covers all major fungal, bacterial, viral, and nematode diseases of potato, with pathogens, symptoms, and management strategies for each — with particular focus on tropical and subtropical climates.

---

## Possible Diseases of Potato

### Fungal and Oomycete Diseases

| Disease                             | Pathogen                                 | Symptoms                                                                                                                                                                              | Treatment / Management                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Late Blight**                     | _Phytophthora infestans_                 | Water-soaked dark green spots on leaf tips and margins; white cottony fungal growth on leaf undersides in humid conditions; tubers develop reddish-brown dry rot internally           | Spray Mancozeb (0.2%) or Ridomil Gold; use BARI-recommended resistant varieties; avoid overhead irrigation; destroy infected haulms immediately after detection          |
| **Early Blight**                    | _Alternaria solani_                      | Small, dark brown "target-board" spots with concentric rings on older, lower leaves; yellowing of surrounding leaf tissue; progresses upward under warm, dry conditions               | Spray Mancozeb (0.2%) or Chlorothalonil; practice crop rotation; ensure balanced potassium (K) fertilization to maintain plant vigour                                    |
| **Black Scurf**                     | _Rhizoctonia solani_                     | Reddish-brown lesions on underground sprouts and stolons; black sclerotia on tuber surfaces; stunted plants with poor emergence                                                       | Treat seeds with Carbendazim (0.1%) or Boric Acid (3%); avoid deep planting; practice 3-year crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops                                    |
| **Fusarium Wilt / Dry Rot**         | _Fusarium_ spp.                          | Yellowing and wilting from bottom up; internal browning of vascular ring visible in cross-section; tubers show sunken, wrinkled skin; dry, shrunken rot inside tubers                 | Use disease-free certified seeds; avoid wounding tubers during harvest; store in cool, ventilated areas; treat seed pieces with Thiram before planting                   |
| **Powdery Scab**                    | _Spongospora subterranea_                | Round, warty galls on tuber skin that rupture to release powdery brown spore masses; root galls visible on seedlings; favoured by waterlogged, heavy soils and cool temperatures      | Use certified disease-free seed; avoid waterlogging and heavy soils; long crop rotation (4+ years); no effective chemical control — prevention is key                    |
| **Silver Scurf**                    | _Helminthosporium solani_                | Silvery-brown discolouration of tuber skin starting at the stolon end; lesions expand in storage causing skin to slough off; tubers shrink due to moisture loss                       | Use disease-free seed; sanitise storage facilities before use; apply Thiabendazole to seed tubers before planting; store at low humidity                                 |
| **Charcoal Rot**                    | _Macrophomina phaseolina_                | Premature vine death in hot, dry weather; grey-black discolouration inside the lower stem; fine black sclerotia visible in vascular tissue; tuber decay under heat and drought stress | Maintain adequate soil moisture; rotate with non-host crops; avoid planting in heavily infested soil; no highly resistant varieties currently available                  |
| **Verticillium Wilt (Early Dying)** | _Verticillium dahliae_ / _V. albo-atrum_ | Premature yellowing and dying of lower leaves; wilting on one side of the plant; brown streaks in vascular tissue; stunted plants and significant yield reduction                     | Use certified disease-free seed; rotate with non-solanaceous crops such as cereals and maize; apply Metham sodium soil fumigant; use resistant varieties where available |

### Bacterial Diseases

| Disease                 | Pathogen / Vector                                                     | Symptoms                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            | Treatment / Management                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bacterial Wilt**      | _Ralstonia solanacearum_                                              | Sudden wilting of entire plant without prior yellowing; milky white bacterial ooze from cut stems placed in water; brown ring visible in tuber vascular bundle                                                                                      | Use certified TPS (True Potato Seed); rogue out infected plants promptly; avoid waterlogging; rotate with maize or wheat                                                                                                                                              |
| **Common Scab**         | _Streptomyces scabiei_                                                | Rough, corky, brownish scabs or pits on tuber skin; surface blemishes reduce marketability but tuber interior remains unaffected                                                                                                                    | Maintain adequate soil moisture during tuber initiation; lower soil pH to 5.2–5.5; avoid heavy liming before or during the potato season                                                                                                                              |
| **Blackleg / Soft Rot** | _Pectobacterium atrosepticum_ / _P. carotovorum_ (formerly _Erwinia_) | Inky black decay of lower stem from the seed piece upward; yellowing and upward rolling of leaves; wet, slimy stem rot in wet weather; dry, shrivelled stems in dry conditions; tuber soft rot in storage. Documented in Bangladeshi potato fields. | Plant only certified, disease-free seed; cut seed pieces with disinfected tools; ensure good field drainage; avoid high soil moisture at planting; store tubers in cool, dry, well-ventilated conditions; no effective chemical control once infection is established |

### Viral Diseases

| Disease                     | Pathogen / Vector                          | Symptoms                                                                                                                                                                                                          | Treatment / Management                                                                                                                                        |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Potato Leaf Roll (PLRV)** | Virus; Vector: Aphids (_Myzus persicae_)   | Leaves roll upward and become stiff and brittle; "net necrosis" visible in vascular tissue of tubers under bright light; stunted growth and yellowing of foliage                                                  | Control aphids with Imidacloprid; use virus-free certified seeds; plant early in the season to escape peak aphid pressure                                     |
| **Potato Virus Y (PVY)**    | Virus; Vector: Aphids                      | Mottling, mosaic patterns, and "vein-banding" on leaves; wrinkled and distorted foliage; significant yield loss especially in secondary infections                                                                | Strict aphid management; use resistant varieties; eliminate weed hosts near the field; source seed from certified virus-tested producers                      |
| **Potato Virus X (PVX)**    | Virus; Vector: Contact / mechanical spread | Mild mosaic or mottle on leaves; often symptomless alone but causes severe combined symptoms when co-infecting with PVY (potato aucuba mosaic); spreads rapidly through contact, contaminated tools, and clothing | Use certified virus-free seed; disinfect tools and equipment between plants; rogue out symptomatic plants; control aphids to limit spread in mixed infections |
| **Potato Virus S (PVS)**    | Virus; Vector: Aphids / contact            | Usually latent or mild — slight deepening of leaf veins, mild rugosity (leaf roughness), slight leaf bronzing; significant yield losses possible without any visible symptoms                                     | Use certified virus-free seed tubers; strict aphid control; rogue infected plants; maintain isolation distances between seed and ware crops                   |

### Nematode Diseases

| Disease                        | Pathogen                                 | Symptoms                                                                                                                                                                                         | Treatment / Management                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Root Knot**                  | _Meloidogyne_ spp.                       | Stunted growth; small warty pimple-like galls on tuber surface; internal necrotic spots visible in cross-section; reduced yield and poor tuber quality                                           | Apply Carbofuran 3G ⚠ (follow local safety regulations strictly); apply Neem cake as soil amendment; practice deep summer ploughing; rotate with marigolds (_Tagetes_ spp.)                                                                                 |
| **Potato Cyst Nematode (PCN)** | _Globodera rostochiensis_ / _G. pallida_ | Patchy yellowing and early dying of plants; severe stunting in heavily infested patches; tiny white, golden, or brown cysts (pinhead-sized) attached to roots; significant tuber yield reduction | Plant only certified nematode-free seed; maintain strict quarantine protocols; rotate with non-solanaceous crops for 6+ years; apply Fenamiphos or Oxamyl nematicide where permitted; trap cropping with susceptible varieties followed by crop destruction |

## Notes

> ⚠ **Carbofuran 3G** is severely restricted or banned in many countries. Always verify local regulatory status before use and strictly follow label instructions.

> ⚠ _Streptomyces scabies_ has been reclassified as _Streptomyces scabiei_ in updated taxonomic literature.

> ✔ For visual disease identification, refer to the **BARI Pictorial Guide**. All chemical treatments must comply with local environmental safety regulations and label dosage instructions.
